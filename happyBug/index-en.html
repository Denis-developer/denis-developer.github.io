Blog

A mistake is not as bad as it may seem. No one on Earth has always been right, this is undeniable. It's not good when people:
know they can make a mistake but attach little importance to it.
repeat regularly their mistakes without learning the lessons.
do not keep control of their work.
do not notice the harm they do to themselves and the others and keep replicating it.
Preventing a mistake is always simpler and cheaper than finding and fixing one. In some cases, such as with space rockets, it’s vitally critical!

Different approaches have been used by famous companies in many countries for years to enhance the quality of their output. They also use error prevention. Probably those who are reading this post have heard of some of these: 6 Sigma, Lean Production, 5S, Kanban, and others.
Let's not get off track: there are companies where an employee who makes a mistake (manufacturing a defective part, failing to keep a deadline critical to the final result of the company’s work, etc.) yet informs the management of it in proper time, will actually get a bonus. Yes, exactly! It's a completely different approach entirely based on the responsibility of all employees, their involvement in the company’s performance which is rewarded by the trust the company places in them. Eventually, it works! The amount of production defects has noticeably decreased, and the number of undetected mistakes is next to zero. The point is to make the smallest possible number of mistakes, understanding that the ones that are made and found can be easily corrected for a positive outcome as opposed to hiding mistakes under the threat of punishment which is the worst that can be done.
